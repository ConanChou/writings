# The Uses of Less

*2026-04-19 · en*

Options are not always good.<br>
Comfort is not always good.<br>
Wealth is not always good.<br>
Freedom is not always good.<br>
Good is not always good.<br>

Obsession can be good.<br>
Constraints can be good.<br>
Obligation can be good.<br>
Struggle can be good.<br>
Bad can be good.<br>

Simplicity, setting constraints,<br>
is the act of removing options,<br>
forcing focus onto what actually matters.<br>

So knowing what matters is the key.<br>
It's what tells us which options are good ones.<br>

---

But how do we come to know what matters?<br>

Rarely through comfort. Comfort lets us drift.<br>
Rarely through wealth or freedom — they scatter us across too many paths, and we learn little about any of them.<br>

We learn what matters by losing things,<br>
or by choosing to give them up.<br>

An obligation shows us who we're willing to answer to.<br>
An obsession shows us what we'd return to even if no one was watching.<br>
A constraint reveals what we'd keep if we could only keep one thing.<br>
Struggle sorts the wants we'll fight for from the ones we only thought we had.<br>

This is the quiet trade: the "bad" things teach us what's good.<br>
And once we know, we can choose our constraints on purpose — not as deprivation, but as direction.<br>

The goal was never to have every option. It was to know which ones were yours.<br>
